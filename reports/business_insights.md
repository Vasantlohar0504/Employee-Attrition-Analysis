# Employee Attrition Business Insights

This report summarizes key insights discovered from the employee attrition analysis.

## Key Findings

### 1. Attrition Rate
The overall employee attrition rate is approximately **16%**, indicating a moderate level of employee turnover.

### 2. Department Attrition
The **Sales department** shows the highest attrition rate compared to HR and R&D.

### 3. Salary Impact
Employees with **lower monthly income** are more likely to leave the organization.

### 4. Overtime Impact
Employees who work **overtime frequently** show a significantly higher attrition rate.

### 5. Work-Life Balance
Low **work-life balance scores** correlate with increased employee turnover.

### 6. Early Career Attrition
Most employees leave within their **first 3 years at the company**.

---

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:

1. Improve work-life balance initiatives.
2. Review compensation policies for lower-income roles.
3. Monitor overtime workload.
4. Focus retention strategies on employees in their first 3 years.
5. Conduct employee engagement surveys in the Sales department.

---

## Conclusion

Employee attrition is influenced by several factors including salary, overtime, work-life balance, and tenure.  
By addressing these areas, organizations can improve employee retention and reduce hiring costs.
